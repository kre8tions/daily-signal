# S1 Writing Quality Report — July 21–22, 2026 (7 most recent editions)

## Scope note and assumptions (autonomous run — flagged per instructions)

1. **What counts as "S1."** Each edition page has two distinct flagship-shaped content units: (a) a page-level "THE SIGNAL" synthesis banner (observation/key-insights/CTA, no byline, no ownedTitle pipeline), and (b) the first individually-bylined, "More"-linked article at the top of the page, which carries `ownedTitle`, a persona byline, a 5-paragraph body, KEY FACTS bullets, and (sometimes) a rendered pull quote — i.e. the actual Pass 1/Pass 2 output described in this skill's rubric. I scored **(b), the first top-of-page bylined article**, as S1, since it is the only unit that matches "owned title / full article body / pull quote / writer persona" from the audit brief. This is a judgment call made because the two units are visually adjacent and easy to conflate.
2. **Archive index vs. archive detail mismatch (data-integrity issue, not a writing-craft issue).** For 4 of the 7 editions checked, the headline shown on `/archive` did not match the content actually rendered at the edition's own `/archive/{date}_{slot}` URL (e.g., the index lists "The Installed Default" for the July 22 evening Digest; the page itself renders "The Shortcut Paradox" — a title that *also* independently renders, with different body text, under the July 22 First Light URL). This was verified two ways (browser DOM read and an independent server-side WebFetch) to rule out a client-side caching artifact. This appears to be a blob-key or synthesis-caching bug in the site, separate from the S1 bylined-article pipeline this audit scores. It does not affect the article-level scores below, which are taken from the live-rendered page a real reader would see, but it is worth a separate look outside this skill's scope.
3. **Persona names.** Individual article bylines (e.g., "Margot," "Bruno") are shown on the article page and were read directly — no inference needed. The roster in `lib/stories.ts` (`WRITERS`, ~65 personas) is much larger than the five illustrative personas named in this skill's brief; I matched each byline to its `inspiration` field from the source file rather than forcing it into one of the five examples.

## Systemic Patterns

### Pattern 1: Pull quote generated but never shown to the reader (6 of 7 articles)
Affected articles: Referee/Margot, Gosling/Arlo, Wolfram/Gene, Kalshi/Rosa, Fantagraphics/Edmund, Cronkite/Bruno.
Root cause: Pass 2 always produces a `pullQuote` (`lib/stories.ts:1979`: "1 sentence lifted verbatim from the shaped body — the single most arresting line... Something a reader would screenshot"), but the front end only renders it for `cardStyle: "full" | "pullquote"`. `CARD_STYLES` (`lib/stories.ts:908`) assigns `"brief"` to 9 of 11 daily slots, so the flagship S1 article frequently lands on `"brief"` and its pull quote is silently dropped.
Example failure: Wolfram/Gene's article generated "It has to cost you something to be wrong. Without that cost, you're reorganizing your ignorance, not reducing it." — arguably the best line of the entire 7-article sample — and no reader ever saw it highlighted.

### Pattern 2: Run-on single-sentence hooks (4 of 7 articles)
Affected articles: Wolfram/Gene (~43 words), Kalshi/Rosa (~52 words), Mummers/Opal (~33 words), Referee/Margot (~27 words).
Root cause: Pass 2's structure instruction (`lib/stories.ts:1968`) reads "para1: EXACTLY 1 sentence — the hook. Irreversible opener. No exceptions." — it constrains sentence *count*, not sentence *length*, so the model satisfies the letter of the instruction with a single overloaded, clause-stacked sentence.
Example failure: "Kalshi is a betting platform that made $300 million during the World Cup by letting people wager on the outcome of matches—and it avoids the roughly 10 to 15 percent tax burden that every other sports betting company pays to states by calling itself a prediction market instead of a gambling operation."

### Pattern 3: Generic "Voice rules" block dilutes the persona's distinct register (4 of 7 articles)
Affected articles: Fantagraphics/Edmund (voice=2), Kalshi/Rosa (voice=3), Cronkite/Bruno (voice=3), Gosling/Arlo (voice=3).
Root cause: Pass 1 concatenates the persona's `style` + `voiceReminder` with a long, generic "Voice rules" block (`lib/stories.ts:1843–1850`, e.g. "Vary sentence length... Vivid and specific... No academic hedging... No semicolons") plus an "EDITORIAL BRIEF" and heavy topic/reference constraints. Nothing in the prompt tells the model to let the persona's register win when it conflicts with the generic rules or the subject matter, so distinctive voices (Sacks's patient case-study compassion, Roxane Gay's fierce personal register) get flattened into a competent-but-anonymous "smart analyst" voice.
Example failure: Edmund is defined as writing with "medical wonder told with patient compassion... never reducing [the subject] to a mechanism" — but his Fantagraphics piece is pure mechanism analysis ("The mechanism was never about merit. It was about infrastructure"), with no trace of the Sacks register.

### Flagged (below the 3-instance pattern threshold, included for severity): Mid-sentence truncation from a decimal/initial-splitting bug (2 of 7 articles)
Affected articles: Cronkite/Bruno ("...on a 1." — should read "...on a 1.3-second delay"), Mummers/Opal ("When folklorists like E." — cuts off mid-name mid-sentence).
Root cause: The sentence-splitting regex in Pass 2's paragraph-shaping step (`lib/stories.ts:2000`, `safe.match(/[^.!?]*[.!?]+["']?\s*/g)`) treats every `.` as a sentence boundary. The abbreviation-protection regex just above it (`ABBREV2`, `lib/stories.ts:1998`) only protects a fixed whitelist of titles ("Mr|Mrs|Ms|Dr|Prof|St|Jr|Sr|vs|etc|No|Vol|pp") — it does not protect single-capital-letter initials (like a person's first initial "E.") or decimal numbers (like "1.3"). When the paragraph-limit slice (`matches.slice(0, limits[k])`) then keeps only the first N pseudo-sentences, everything after the false split is silently discarded. This is a code-level regex defect, not a prompt-wording issue, but it sits directly in the Pass 2 pipeline and reaches the published page twice in this 7-article sample — flagged with an exact fix below given how visibly broken the resulting sentence is to a reader.

## Proposed Prompt Fixes

### Fix 1: Pull quote pipeline output never reaches the reader
**Pass:** 2 (rendering gate downstream of the Pass 2 `pullQuote` field — not itself LLM prompt wording)
**Field:** `CARD_STYLES` constant, `lib/stories.ts:908`
**Current instruction:** `const CARD_STYLES: Story["cardStyle"][] = ["full", "pullquote", "brief", "brief", "brief", "brief", "brief", "brief", "brief", "brief", "brief"];`
**Proposed addition:** Force the S1 slot (the first bylined article of each edition) to always draw `"full"` or `"pullquote"` from this array regardless of rotation position — e.g. reserve index 0 of the per-edition slot assignment specifically for the flagship story: `const CARD_STYLES_FOR_S1: Story["cardStyle"] = seededRandom(refSeed) < 0.5 ? "full" : "pullquote";` applied only when `slotIndex === 0`, leaving the existing rotation for S2 onward.
**Would have improved:** Referee/Margot, Gosling/Arlo, Wolfram/Gene, Kalshi/Rosa, Fantagraphics/Edmund, Cronkite/Bruno (6 of 7 articles).
**Risk:** If `"full"` and `"pullquote"` cardStyles carry additional layout weight (larger hero image, different CTA slot), forcing S1 into one of them every day removes visual variety across editions — worth confirming with a design pass, but the current behavior (writing a screenshot-quality line and hiding it 6 times out of 7) is strictly worse for the product's core sharing mechanic.

### Fix 2: No word-count ceiling on the one-sentence hook
**Pass:** 2
**Field:** Structure instruction, `lib/stories.ts:1968`
**Current instruction:** `"- para1: EXACTLY 1 sentence — the hook. Irreversible opener. No exceptions."`
**Proposed addition:** `"- para1: EXACTLY 1 sentence, 25 words or fewer — the hook. Irreversible opener. If the natural draft sentence exceeds 25 words, cut it at the strongest clause boundary (em-dash, 'because', 'which', 'and') and move the remainder into para2 as a second sentence. No exceptions."`
**Would have improved:** Wolfram/Gene, Kalshi/Rosa, Mummers/Opal, Referee/Margot (4 of 7 articles).
**Risk:** Some genuinely strong hooks are single long sentences with an internal turn (e.g. "X, but Y"); a hard 25-word ceiling could force an artificial break in a sentence that was actually working. Mitigate by keeping the instruction as a strong default rather than a hard validator-enforced cutoff (unlike the no-colon rule on `ownedTitle`, which the code already enforces programmatically at `lib/stories.ts:1939`).

### Fix 3: Generic "Voice rules" block outweighs the persona's stated register
**Pass:** 1
**Field:** "Voice rules" block, `lib/stories.ts:1843–1850`
**Current instruction:** `"Voice rules:\n- Vary sentence length. Short punches. Then one that earns it. Then short again.\n- Vivid and specific — name the thing, don't describe it abstractly.\n- When the source contains a striking number — use it. The specific number does more work than the abstraction.\n- No academic hedging...\n- No throat-clearing openers...\n- Never reference the source article or your own process...\n- No semicolons — ever. Rewrite as two sentences."`
**Proposed addition:** Insert as the first line of the "Voice rules" block, before the existing bullets: `"- Persona first: every rule below is subordinate to your persona's voice and voiceReminder above. Where a generic rule and your persona's register would pull the sentence in different directions, follow the persona. Before finishing, reread your draft and confirm at least 2 sentences are ones only this persona — not a generic sharp analyst — would have written."`
**Would have improved:** Fantagraphics/Edmund, Kalshi/Rosa, Cronkite/Bruno, Gosling/Arlo (4 of 7 articles).
**Risk:** Persona-forward writing can drift from the "intelligent, not ideological" and named-case grounding requirements stated earlier in the same prompt (`lib/stories.ts:1834, 1841`) if taken too far — the self-check framing ("at least 2 sentences") is meant to keep this additive rather than a full rewrite of the existing constraints.

### Fix 4: Mid-sentence truncation from unprotected decimal points and initials
**Pass:** 2
**Field:** Abbreviation-protection regex, `lib/stories.ts:1998`
**Current instruction (code, not prose):** `const ABBREV2 = /\b(Mr|Mrs|Ms|Dr|Prof|St|Jr|Sr|vs|etc|No|Vol|pp)\./g;`
**Proposed addition:** Extend the regex to also protect single-capital-letter initials and decimal numbers before the sentence-split runs: `const ABBREV2 = /\b(Mr|Mrs|Ms|Dr|Prof|St|Jr|Sr|vs|etc|No|Vol|pp)\.|\b[A-Z]\.(?=\s?[A-Z])|(?<=\d)\.(?=\d)/g;` — the second alternative protects initials like "E." when followed by another capitalized token (a name), and the third protects decimal numbers like "1.3" from being read as a sentence end.
**Would have improved:** Cronkite/Bruno, Mummers/Opal (2 of 7 articles — below this audit's 3-instance pattern threshold, included because the resulting sentences are visibly broken to any reader).
**Risk:** The initials pattern (`\b[A-Z]\.(?=\s?[A-Z])`) could over-match a genuine sentence that ends with a single capitalized word followed by another capitalized sentence-starter (rare but possible) — recommend spot-checking a sample of generated articles after deploying before treating it as fully solved.

### Fix 5 (minor, below pattern threshold — noted for the record): No instruction against ending on a rhetorical question
**Pass:** 1
**Field:** Ending instructions, `lib/stories.ts:1869` (the "FORBIDDEN" list)
**Current instruction:** Lists forbidden endings ("vague endings that restate the opening... endings that stay inside the subject world...") but does not forbid ending on a question.
**Proposed addition:** Add to the same FORBIDDEN list: `"; ending the piece on a question — the final sentence must be a declarative claim, even if the paragraph before it poses a question to build toward it."`
**Would have improved:** Mummers/Opal (1 of 7 articles — below the 3-instance threshold; noted because it is an explicit, named, automatic score-1 disqualifier in this skill's rubric and a one-line fix).
**Risk:** None significant — this narrows an already-existing FORBIDDEN list rather than adding new constraints elsewhere.

## What's Working

- **Headlines and closings are the two strongest dimensions on average** (3.43 each). When the pipeline isn't fighting a rendering or truncation bug, the model reliably produces sharp, tension-bearing titles ("Fantagraphics Built Prestige, Diamond Distributors Built Poverty"; "Cronkite's Uncertainty Was the Content, Not the Ritual") and aphoristic, standalone closing lines (Wolfram/Gene's "It has to cost you something to be wrong. Without that cost, you're reorganizing your ignorance, not reducing it." is a 5/5 closing and arguably the single best sentence in the sample).
