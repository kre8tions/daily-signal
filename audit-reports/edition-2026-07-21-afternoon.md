## Proposed Fixes

### Fix 1: Add a reader-application clause to brief summaries
**Pass:** 1
**Field:** brief-card summary/body generation prompt (S2–S9)
**Current:** missing — no instruction found requiring the summary to bridge to reader application; current output is descriptive/analytical only.
**Proposed:** "End the summary with a clause that gives the reader something to do, think, or watch for — not just what happened, but what it means for them going forward. Example pattern: 'This means...' or 'Watch for...' Keep it to one added clause, not a new sentence, to preserve brief-card brevity."
**Would have improved:** S3, S4, S5, S6, S7, S9
**Risk:** Risk of a formulaic "watch for X" tag appended mechanically to every brief, which would flatten distinctiveness across stories if not varied; may also push some briefs past their intended one-sentence length.

### Fix 2: Always populate a pull quote
**Pass:** 1 (briefs) and 2 (S1 full article)
**Field:** pull-quote/highlight-sentence field
**Current:** missing or unpopulated for 8 of 9 stories.
**Proposed:** "Select one self-contained sentence (under 25 words) from the body as the pull quote and always render it on the card. If no existing sentence qualifies on its own, write one within this field that would work standalone."
**Would have improved:** S1, S3, S4, S5, S6, S7, S8, S9
**Risk:** For one-sentence briefs (S3, S4), the "pull quote" would just duplicate the only sentence already shown, adding visual redundancy rather than value — needs a minimum-content-length gate so it only fires when the body has more than one candidate sentence, or is allowed to just re-render the single sentence as the quote by design.

### Fix 3: Cross-check ownedTitle against the summary's actual subject
**Pass:** 1
**Field:** ownedTitle generation prompt
**Current:** missing — no consistency constraint between ownedTitle and summary observed.
**Proposed:** "The ownedTitle must reference the same specific claim, subject, time period, and setting as the summary field. Do not title the story using a historical aside, comparison, or tangential detail from the source article if the summary's actual focus is elsewhere."
**Would have improved:** S3 (Europe/binoculars title vs. Southeast Asia contest body), S6 (40%-statistic title vs. single-artist anecdote body)
**Risk:** Could over-constrain legitimate creative titling that deliberately leads with a vivid historical hook before the summary narrows the focus — needs to allow a hook-then-narrow structure as long as both fields share the same core subject.

### Fix 4: Require a named example in brief summaries
**Pass:** 1
**Field:** brief-card summary/body generation prompt
**Current:** missing.
**Proposed:** "Where the source article names a specific person, product, organization, or place, the summary must include at least one such named example rather than a generic reference ('artists,' 'a report,' 'officials')."
**Would have improved:** S3, S4
**Risk:** Some source articles genuinely report aggregate/statistical findings with no single named example available — forcing one could introduce an inaccurate or fabricated specific.

### Fix 5: Require a claim-bearing headline, not a fact count
**Pass:** 1
**Field:** ownedTitle generation prompt
**Current:** missing.
**Proposed:** "If the summary contains a counter-intuitive claim or named mechanism ('X happens not because of A, but because of B'), the ownedTitle must surface that claim rather than a neutral fact or count. Reject titles that are pure trivia statements (e.g., 'Five Films Shot on X') when a stronger claim exists in the same story's summary."
**Would have improved:** S8 ("Nolan's Five Films Shot on IMAX Cameras" scored 2 on Headline despite its own summary containing a strong "self-fulfilling prophecy" thesis)
**Risk:** Claim-forward titles can run long or feel breathless if not capped; should pair with a character/word limit.
