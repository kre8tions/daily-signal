## Proposed Fixes

### Fix 1: Ban unresolved endings
**Pass:** 2
**Field:** full article body prompt — closing-paragraph instruction
**Current:** Missing (or not being enforced) — inferred from evidence, since 3 of 9 stories in this single edition end on an unfinished contrast or a stacked rhetorical question.
**Proposed:** "The final sentence must be a complete, standalone declarative statement that lands an idea beyond today's news. Never end on a question mark. Never write a contrast setup ('The question isn't whether X...' / 'Not Y, but...') without completing both halves in the same sentence."
**Would have improved:** S5, S6, S9 (and tightens the soft/fragment endings on S3 and S7).
**Risk:** If over-applied, the model may over-explain contrasts that were intentionally left punchy and terse — the instruction should target completion, not verbosity.

### Fix 2: Guardrail on sentence fragments
**Pass:** 2
**Field:** full article body prompt — sentence-construction / house-style instruction
**Current:** Likely permits short fragments for rhythm with no check that each fragment carries its own subject (inferred from repeated dropped-subject fragments across 3 stories).
**Proposed:** "Fragments are allowed for emphasis, but each one must be readable as a complete thought on its own. Never start a sentence with 'Means...' or otherwise drop the subject of a clause — if a fragment needs the previous sentence to parse grammatically, add the subject back in."
**Would have improved:** S5, S9 (readability), and indirectly S8's garbled numeric sentence if the same pass also checks that inserted figures/comparisons are complete.
**Risk:** Could sand down some of the staccato rhythm that gives the house style its punch if enforced too literally — should target grammatical completeness, not fragment use itself.

### Fix 3: Cap verbatim self-repetition
**Pass:** 2
**Field:** full article body prompt — mechanism-emphasis instruction
**Current:** Appears to allow (or encourage) restating the core insight later in the piece with no instruction against repeating it word-for-word.
**Proposed:** "If you restate the article's core insight later in the piece for emphasis, rephrase it — never repeat an identical sentence verbatim. Word-for-word repetition reads as an editing error, not emphasis."
**Would have improved:** S2, S8, S9 (voice freshness, readability).
**Risk:** Minimal — this only tightens an existing device rather than removing it.

### Fix 4: Make pull-quote selection mandatory for every story
**Pass:** 1
**Field:** ownedTitle/summary/bullets prompt — quote-selection step
**Current:** Unclear/inconsistent — only 2 of 9 stories in this edition (S2, S4) surfaced a displayed pull quote; the other 7 had usable candidates in the generated body that were never selected.
**Proposed:** "Every story, regardless of cardStyle, must select exactly one pull quote from the generated article body — a self-contained sentence, ideally under 25 words, that could stand alone as a social post. This step runs independent of cardStyle and independent of whether Key Insights/Key Facts bullets are also generated."
**Would have improved:** S1, S3, S5, S6, S7, S8, S9 — 7 of 9 stories, and would lift the edition's weakest-but-one dimension (Pull Quote, 3.1 avg).
**Risk:** If no line in a given article genuinely stands alone under 25 words, forcing a selection could surface a weaker quote than none at all — allow a fallback to the single best available candidate rather than a hard word-count gate.
