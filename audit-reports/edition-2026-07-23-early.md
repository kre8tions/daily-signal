## Edition-wide patterns

### Pattern 1: List fragmented into a sentence fragment by stray period (2 stories: S1, S3)
- S1: "...because fewer choices were legally, financially. Socially available to them."
- S3: "...while a signed artist's finances are itemized, witnessed. Available for prosecutors to weaponize."

Root cause (Pass 1 + a Pass-2 post-process step, both in `C:\dev\daily-signal\lib\stories.ts`):
- Pass 1 body-generation prompt (line 1853, reinforced at line 1872) instructs: `"No semicolons — ever. Rewrite as two sentences."` / `"colons anywhere in the prose — rewrite as two sentences, no exceptions; semicolons — rewrite as two sentences, no exceptions"`. This gives the model no guidance for how to render a natural 3-item list ("legally, financially, and socially") without a semicolon, and it sometimes drops the "and" and inserts a period mid-list instead.
- Separately, `repairPunctuation()` (lines 1409–1448) runs an LLM rewrite on any sentence still containing a colon/semicolon after Pass 1, with the instruction: `"Rewrite each sentence below to remove colons and semicolons. Split into two sentences at the colon or semicolon — no colons, no semicolons in the output. Keep the meaning intact."` This instruction has no requirement that both resulting halves be independently grammatical — a mechanical split of "...legally, financially; and socially available to them" produces exactly the observed fragment.

### Pattern 2: Closing paragraph violates an existing, explicit closing rule (2 stories: S5, S6)
- S5 violates the Pass 1 forbidden-list rule (line 1872): `"endings that stay inside the subject world — the final paragraph must connect to something the reader can see in their own creative practice, career, or way of thinking, not just a conclusion about the subject itself."` S5's ending never leaves Apple's product strategy.
- S6 violates the Pass 2 para5 rule (line 1975): `"Never write a contrast setup ('The question isn't whether X...' / 'Not Y, but...') without completing both halves in the same sentence."` S6 ends on "This is not a choice between two paths" — a negation with no completion.

Both are cases of an **ignored instruction**, not a missing one — the rules already exist and are specific; they simply weren't enforced on these two drafts.

### Pattern 3: Flat, fact-first hooks (2 stories: S4, S5)
S4 and S5 both open with a neutral news-announcement sentence (chart count; spec bump) rather than fusing the claim into sentence one, unlike S2/S3/S7 which name the subject and plant the tension in the same first sentence. Root cause: Pass 1 line 1826 instructs `"Your first move is always to establish the subject: name the thing, anchor the reader, give them a foothold. Then have a real opinion about it."` — phrased as two sequential moves, this gives the model permission to spend sentence 1 purely on subject-naming and defer the claim, which is exactly what happened in S4 and S5.

### Pattern 4: Pull quote silently missing despite being a required field (2 stories: S3, S6)
Both S3 and S6 have `header2` values that differ from their opening subheads (confirming the Pass 2 shaping call succeeded), but no `pullQuote` was surfaced on either published page, even though strong candidate sentences exist in both bodies. The Pass 2 prompt (line 1982) marks `pullQuote` as a required output field with no fallback if the model omits it — `extractedPullQuote` (line 1953) has no other source, since the earlier metadata-extraction pass (lines 1900–1910) never requests a `pullQuote` field at all.

### Pattern 5: Persona-voice fidelity drift on topic mismatch (2 of 7 stories clearly affected: S3, S6)
Writer assignment (`getWriterAssignments`, line 1355) is purely randomized per date/slot with no topic-affinity weighting — so a persona like Dawn (Mary Oliver — natural-world attention, "a single pond, a single season, a single creature") can land on a bodybuilding-business-history story (S6) with zero natural-world content, or Clive (Bill Bryson — self-deprecating, dinner-table delight) can land on a murder-case financial-motive analysis (S3). This is expected by design, but the compensating instruction — Pass 1 line 1844: `"Persona first... Before finishing, reread your draft and confirm at least 2 sentences are ones only this persona — not a generic sharp analyst — would have written"` — did not produce a recognizable Bryson or Oliver fingerprint in either piece; both read as generic sharp-analyst prose.

## Proposed Fixes

### Fix 1: List-fragment sentences
**Pass:** 1 (body generation) and the `repairPunctuation()` post-process step
**Field:** Voice rules block, line 1853/1872; and the rewrite prompt inside `repairPunctuation()`, line 1431
**Current:** `"No semicolons — ever. Rewrite as two sentences."` (Pass 1) / `"Rewrite each sentence below to remove colons and semicolons. Split into two sentences at the colon or semicolon — no colons, no semicolons in the output. Keep the meaning intact."` (repairPunctuation)
**Proposed:** Add to both: `"If the colon or semicolon separates parallel items in a list rather than two independent clauses, do NOT split into two sentences — rewrite the list using commas and 'and' instead (e.g., 'legally, financially, and socially available' — never 'legally, financially. Socially available'). Only split into two sentences when both resulting halves are independently complete, grammatical sentences on their own."`
**Would have improved:** S1, S3
**Risk:** Very low — this is a grammar-correctness clarification, not a style constraint.

### Fix 2: Endings that stay inside the subject world
**Pass:** 1
**Field:** Forbidden-list instruction, line 1872
**Current:** `"endings that stay inside the subject world — the final paragraph must connect to something the reader can see in their own creative practice, career, or way of thinking, not just a conclusion about the subject itself"` — already present, but ignored on S5.
**Proposed:** Add a hard self-check ahead of the forbidden list: `"Before finishing, check your last paragraph specifically: does it name something outside today's subject that the reader can act on or see differently in their own life? If the last paragraph could be published in a product-news trade blog with only the subject swapped out, it has failed this check — rewrite it."`
**Would have improved:** S5
**Risk:** Low — could add a sentence to very short brief-style pieces, which is the intended fix.

### Fix 3: Incomplete contrast-setup endings
**Pass:** 2 (structure-shaping call)
**Field:** para5 instruction, line 1975
**Current:** `"Never write a contrast setup ('The question isn't whether X...' / 'Not Y, but...') without completing both halves in the same sentence."` — already present, ignored on S6.
**Proposed:** Extend the pattern match explicitly: `"This includes negations without a positive completion — 'This is not X.' with no following clause stating what it IS. Every sentence of this shape must read 'This is not X, it's Y' in one sentence, never split across two with the second half left unwritten."`
**Would have improved:** S6
**Risk:** Low.

### Fix 4: Flat fact-first hooks
**Pass:** 1
**Field:** Reader-orientation instruction, line 1826
**Current:** `"Your first move is always to establish the subject: name the thing, anchor the reader, give them a foothold. Then have a real opinion about it."`
**Proposed:** `"Your first sentence must do both jobs at once — name the subject AND plant the claim or tension in the same sentence. Do not spend sentence 1 purely on neutral fact-establishment and defer the opinion to sentence 2 or paragraph 2 (e.g., not 'Apple is preparing a MacBook Neo update with more RAM' followed later by the real claim — fuse them: 'Apple's MacBook Neo update looks like a spec bump; it's margin protection.')"`
**Would have improved:** S4, S5
**Risk:** Medium — for genuinely low-drama subjects this could force an artificial-feeling twist into sentence 1; keep as strong guidance rather than a hard rule.

### Fix 5: Pull quote silently missing
**Pass:** 2 (structure-shaping call)
**Field:** pullQuote output field, line 1982, plus the fallback logic at line 1953/2017
**Current:** `"pullQuote: 1 sentence lifted verbatim from the shaped body — the single most arresting line. Something a reader would screenshot."` — marked required in the prompt text but not enforced; `extractedPullQuote` has no fallback source if the model omits it.
**Proposed prompt addition:** `"This field is required and must never be an empty string — if you are unsure which sentence is strongest, pick the most counter-intuitive or emotionally resonant sentence in para4 or para5 rather than leaving it blank."` Also recommend (code-level, outside this skill's scope but worth flagging): if `scaffold.pullQuote` is empty after Pass 2, fall back to re-requesting just that field rather than publishing with none.
**Would have improved:** S3, S6
**Risk:** Low.

### Fix 6: Persona-voice fidelity on topic mismatch
**Pass:** 1
**Field:** Voice rules, line 1844
**Current:** `"Persona first... Before finishing, reread your draft and confirm at least 2 sentences are ones only this persona — not a generic sharp analyst — would have written."`
**Proposed:** `"If today's subject has no obvious overlap with your persona's usual territory, do not default to generic analyst prose — find the persona's characteristic MOVE (not their usual subject matter) and apply it here. E.g. Dawn's move is sustained attention to one small thing until it opens into something larger — apply that attention-structure to any subject, not just nature. Clive's move is delight at a disproportionate consequence hiding inside something small — apply that structure to a legal argument, not just history."`
**Would have improved:** S3, S6 (and is a likely recurring issue across other editions given random persona assignment)
**Risk:** Low, though this is harder to verify mechanically than the other fixes — recommend spot-checking after the change rather than assuming it lands.
